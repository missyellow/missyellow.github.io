Community gambling

A series of gambling machines designed to raise community participation. Gambling is often very destructive to a community with bettering shops proliferating the high street working to remove wealth from the area.

These gambling machines were designed to offer an alternative with profits gained from a betting shop being driven back into the area. The machines also offered the chance to gamble on local events rather than national events.

The area chosen for these prototypes was Peckham in south London. participants were offered the opportunity to gamble on the likely hood of sirens been heard at specific locations and of the the likely timing of a natural event.
