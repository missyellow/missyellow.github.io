Every day machines

Work in progress.

Designing machines to play with life. These machines are experiments for different ways of performing actions and tasks. The machines are designed for deliberately obtuse scenarios giving unexpected consequences. They are part of my attempt at becoming super human through the development of devices.

Eating pasta

Pasta is ubiquitous worldwide and renowned for its taste and style with which its is eaten, almost everybody forming an opinion and favouring there own way. In my opinion pasta should not be over cooked and each strand of spaghetti should have a slightly roughed surface and be coated with an even layer of source. This is a machine that addresses those points.

Points of operation

1. Cooked pasta is placed into the feed bowl, whilst the source hopper is filled with a cooked source of choice.

2. A motor pushes the pasta down the tube towards the source hopper, knurled cogs roughen the surface of the spaghetti on the way.

2. Source is gravity feed from the hopper and coats the surface of the spaghetti, giving an even coat.

3. The spaghetti is lastly accelerated out of the mouth piece.

Listening Aids

The brain has evolved to give greater importance to some sounds rather than to others. General and familiar sounds often become benign and go unacknowledged.

The listening aids aim to demonstrate that the sound world can be as rich as the visual world. They provide augmentation to our hearing with filters and enhancements to certain sound bands. The devices provide presets for hidden sounds, high pitch sounds and rhythmical sounds.

The Aids are small and light enough to be portable so they can be taken out on general day-to-day activities.
