Life with Fish

<

This is a project that attempts to enhance our empathy with aquatic life, to explore and open up the hidden soundscape of the oceans.

It appears to me that people don’t share the same empathy towards aquatic life as they feel for land animals. I think that this attitude arrises from the differing ways that humans experience the water and underwater habitat compared to aquatic lifeforms. Through my project I am aiming to remove some of these barriers, by developing a new way of listening to and experiencing sounds within the ocean, closer to how aquatic life its self experiences sound.

Many fish are soniferous, and a rich and wide sound scape exists underwater. Using passive sonar technologies incorporated within devices, such as a head piece or a body patches, I would like to make the experience of swimming with aquatic life as noisy as listening to bird song on land.

Once I have developed my system, I hope to use it to build empathy and communications with dolphins, perhaps the most well known and loved noisy aquatic inhabitant.
